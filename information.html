🩸 DIABETES

The only definitive way to know if you have diabetes is through official diagnostic blood tests, such as an A1C, fasting plasma glucose, or oral glucose tolerance test. You should consult a doctor to get tested if you experience hallmark warning signs, which frequently include:

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination: The body’s attempt to flush excess sugar through urine leads to dehydration and an urgent need to drink more fluids.
  • Unexplained weight loss: Despite being hungrier than usual, muscles and fat are burned for energy because the body cannot access glucose.
  • Persistent fatigue: Lack of energy occurs when cells are unable to receive and utilize sugar for fuel.
  • Blurred vision: High blood sugar can cause the fluid in the eyes to swell, altering your ability to focus.
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections: Elevated blood sugar levels can damage circulation and impair immune function.
  • Numbness or tingling: Nerve damage (neuropathy) often surfaces as tingling or "pins and needles" in the hands and feet.

⚕️ HYPERTENSION

High blood pressure (hypertension) is called the "silent killer" because it usually has no noticeable symptoms. The only definitive way to know if you have it is by getting your blood pressure checked using an inflatable cuff.

    If your blood pressure spikes to dangerously high levels (180/120 mm Hg or higher), or if it has been elevated for years, you may occasionally experience:
  • Severe headachesShortness of breath.
  • Blurred or double vision.
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness.
  • Nose bleeds.
  • Chest pain or an irregular heartbeat.
    What Your Readings Mean. A blood pressure reading consists of two numbers—systolic (top number, pressure when your heart beats) and diastolic (bottom number, pressure when your heart is resting). According to the American Heart Association and the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute, blood pressure categories are classified as:
  • Normal: Less than 120/80 mm Hg.
  • Elevated: 120-129 systolic and less than 80 diastolic.
  • Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139 systolic or 80-89 diastolic.
  • Stage 2 Hypertension: 140 or higher systolic or 90 or higher diastolic.
  • Hypertensive Crisis: Higher than 180 systolic and/or higher than 120 diastolic (Requires immediate medical attention)
